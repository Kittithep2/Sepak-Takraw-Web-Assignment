Sepak Takraw

Sepak takraw is a volleyball-like game that originated in Southeast Asia.
The literal translation of ‘Kick Ball’ is a team sport played with a ball made of rattan
the grapefruit-sized balls are traditionally handwoven from bamboo or rattan,
but modern ones are usually synthetic. Between two teams of three players on a badminton court with a 1.5-metre-tall net.

It is very similar to volleyball, but players use their feet and head to touch the ball, no hands. Popular across Southeast Asia, it is particularly popular in Malaysia where it is locally known as 'Sepak Raga' and Thailand where it is called 'Takraw'.


Object of the Game

Is for one team to score more points than the opponent by causing their opponents to commit a fault, resulting in a score being awarded.

Winning the Game

A game of Sepak Takraw Is won by the first side to win two sets each set is won by the team that is first to reach 21 points Basic rules and scoring for sepak takraw are similar to volleyball.
Each team is allowed a maximum of 3 touches of the ball to pass it over the other side – without letting it touch the ground.
